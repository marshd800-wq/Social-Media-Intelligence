## The one-paragraph headline

Diana's audience rewards **story, specificity, and a clear next step** — and quietly punishes **generic travel/seasonal filler**. Her best posts are **reels and carousels** with a **curiosity or POV hook**, a **Metro-Atlanta-specific** point of view, a **personal or client-transformation angle**, **very few hashtags**, and a **one-word comment CTA**. Her worst posts are **static image graphics** running the **"Find Home Anywhere" global series** or **seasonal home-tips**, stuffed with **5+ hashtags** and no CTA. The move going forward: more reels + carousels rooted in Atlanta and in Diana's own voice, and a hard cut to the global/seasonal graphics.

## 2. Top 10 hooks & opening lines by performance

Use these as templates — the structure travels even when the topic changes.

1. **"Nothing brings me more joy than seeing my childhood friends take their first step…"** — personal-story + client-win *(perf 97, 1,102 reach, 35 comments)*
2. **"🚨Metro Atlanta teachers🚨, this one is for you."** — call out a local segment + give value *(perf 95, 46 comments — her most-commented post ever)*
3. **"The job offer was the easy part."** — curiosity gap, bold one-liner, relocation *(perf 82)*
4. **"This is what selling a luxury home in Metro Atlanta actually looks like."** — "what it actually looks like" + local + luxury *(perf 82)*
5. **"POV: you want way more space for your money."** — POV framing + buyer desire *(perf 80)*
6. **"opens piggy bank 'Alright, time to buy the whole block…'"** — humor / skit cold open *(perf 80, 893 reach)*
7. **"How it's going vs how it started…"** — transformation format *(perf 77)*
8. **"You don't have to know everything about real estate — that's my job."** — reassurance + authority *(perf 76)*
9. **"Been in your home 3–5 years? Do NOT make a move until you read this."** — direct address + warning + save-bait *(top-decile saves)*
10. **"Describe your job and make it sound illegal… I'll go first."** — pattern-interrupt humor *(perf-strong, 618 reach)*

**Hook rules that separate winners from losers:**
- Open with a **curiosity gap, a POV, or a bold <9-word line** — never "May is here, and with it comes the promise of…"
- Name the place: **"Metro Atlanta," "Cobb," "Marietta," "Kennesaw"** beats "your city."
- Call out **one person** ("teachers," "move-up sellers," "first-time buyers," "relocating professionals").
- Winning captions use the language: `Metro Atlanta`, `first time (buyers)`, `trusted`, `built`, `opportunity`, `budget`, `what's next`, `grateful`. Losing captions use travelogue + seasonal filler.

## 3. Formats to post MORE of

1. **Reels** — your reach engine (avg reach 250, ~2× static). Anchor the top of the funnel.
2. **Carousels** — your engagement + save engine (ER 0.14, highest of any format; strong for "3–5 things," myth-busting, client-win reveals).
3. **Personal-story / behind-the-scenes reels** — highest comment rate; the "Pulse" in Luxury With a Pulse.
4. **POV & humor cold-opens** — cheap to make, highest reach ceiling, brings in non-followers.
5. **Value carousels with a one-word CTA** — "Comment DEAL / NEXT / MOVE" posts earn **~2.3× the comments** and feed the DM funnel.

## 4. Content to STOP or post LESS of

1. **The "Find Home Anywhere" global series** (Canada/Bahamas/UAE/Italy…). 19 posts, avg perf **40**, avg reach **84**, near-zero comments. It dilutes the local-expert positioning. **Retire it or cut to an occasional aspirational reel — not a monthly static graphic.**
2. **Generic seasonal / home-tips graphics** (Thanksgiving turkey, coffee-table styling, "winter prep"). Worst theme, avg reach **22**. If a seasonal idea is worth doing, make it a **reel in Diana's voice about an Atlanta home**, not a stock graphic.
3. **Static image posts as a default.** 8 of the bottom 10 are static. Reserve static for the rare announcement; lead with reels and carousels.
4. **Hashtag stuffing.** Bottom posts average **5.7** hashtags; top posts average **1.4**. Cap at **3 relevant, local hashtags**.
5. **Platitude captions** ("owning a home is more than a roof over your head"). No searchable question, no story — they flatline.
